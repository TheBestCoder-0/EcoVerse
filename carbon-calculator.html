Why Carbon Footprint Matters

A carbon footprint measures the total amount of greenhouse gases (GHGs) emitted into the atmosphere as a result of human activity. By calculating your carbon footprint, you can better understand the impact of your lifestyle and take steps to reduce your environmental footprint.

Every action, from the energy you use at home to the transportation you rely on, contributes to your carbon footprint. The goal is to reduce these emissions to help mitigate climate change.

Tips to Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

Reducing your carbon footprint is one of the most impactful ways you can contribute to the fight against climate change. Here are some actions you can take:

Switch to Public Transport

Reduce the carbon emissions from your personal car by using public transport, walking, or cycling. Consider carpooling or using electric vehicles if possible.

Switch to Renewable Energy

Install solar panels or opt for energy providers that use renewable energy sources like wind and solar to power your home.

Recycle and Reduce Waste

Recycling reduces waste and conserves resources. Composting organic waste can also reduce methane emissions from landfills.
